Accessibility, Accommodation and Adaptive Computer Technology (AAACT)

Government of Canada Accessibility Training and Events

The Government of Canada’s vision is to make Canada’s public service the most accessible and inclusive in the world.

Shared Services Canada (SSC) supports this vision by making information and communications technology usable by all. SSC hosts many training and events related to accessibility and they are listed on this page.

To view previous presentations check out our past presentations page.

To help promote upcoming accessibility activities organized by various Government of Canada departments that are open to public service employees we have also posted their event details below.

  • Developing an ICT Accessibility Procurement Strategy

    • Presented by Shared Services Canada

    Shared Services Canada (SSC) is hosting the second installment of a three part special learning series on procuring accessible Information and Communication Technology (ICT).

    Join us to learn about the key elements that Business Owners, (client department or agency, Technical Authority) and Contracting Authorities must consider when developing a procurement strategy that includes ICT accessibility.

    Learn how to help the Government of Canada reach its goal of becoming the most accessible public service in the world.

    Additional resources:

    If you have any questions or require accommodation for this session, please contact the AAACT Events team by 24 May, 2024 so we can make arrangements in advance.

    • Download the PowerPoint presentation before the session here: Developing an ICT accessibility procurement strategy (PPT, 4.527 MB)

    SSC is your partner in ICT accessibility.

    If you have any questions, please contact the AAACT program.

  • Document Accessibility Workshop

    • Presented by The Accessibility, Accommodation and Adaptive Computer Technology (AAACT) program at Shared Services Canada

    Learn how to apply the basic principles of accessibility to produce accessible content in various formats (ex. Word documents, PDF, PowerPoint). Hosted by AAACT program staff, this workshop will:

    • highlight the importance of accessibility
    • enable participants to identify document accessibility issues
    • demonstrate how participants can perform document accessibility checks and fix content
    • include a combination of presentations, demonstrations, exercises and facilitated discussion
    • how accessible documents are faster and easier to convert into other formats

    If you have any questions, please contact the AAACT program.

  • How to consider and integrate accessibility in procurement

    • Presented by Shared Services Canada and Public Service and Procurement Canada

    Federal procurement professionals are encouraged to join Public Services and Procurement Canada and Shared Services Canada at this introductory session on what it means to consider and integrate accessibility in federal procurement.

    At this core session, participants will learn what is meant by accessible procurement, key definitions and supporting legislation, the role of business owners (client department or agency, Technical Authority) and Contracting Authorities in considering accessibility in federal procurement, the steps to consider and integrate accessibility in procurement; and where to find resources and help.

    The session will end with a question and answer period.

    Participants are encouraged to join the Agents of Change for Accessible Procurement by email, an interdepartmental community of practice (CoP). This CoP is an excellent way to stay informed on emerging best practices and standards that help to advance accessibility in federal procurement.

    If you have any questions or require accommodation for this session, please contact the AAACT Events team by 17 May, 2024 so we can make arrangements in advance.

    • Download the PowerPoint presentation before the session here: How to consider and integrate accessibility in procurement (PPT, 664 KB)

    If you have any questions, please contact the AAACT program.

  • Introduction to Procuring Accessible Information and Communication Technology (ICT)

    • Presented by Shared Services Canada (SSC)

    Shared Services Canada (SSC) is hosting the first installment of a three part special learning series on procuring accessible Information and Communication Technology (ICT) that will help you acquire the necessary skills to be accessible and inclusive in your procurement.

    Join us to learn how to incorporate the Government of Canada’s (GC) recommended ICT accessibility standard in a procurement to ensure the GC procures accessible ICT goods and services. This includes the purchase of software, hardware, e-learning, and consulting services which require the production of a report and/or other kinds of ICT.

    Learn how to help the Government of Canada reach its goal of becoming the most accessible public service in the world.

  • Overview of accessibility features on smartphones and adaptive technology in the workplace

    • Presented by The Accessibility, Accommodation and Adaptive Computer Technology (AAACT) program at Shared Services Canada

    This learning activity includes three segments: two presentations and a questions and answers session.

    The Accessibility, Accommodations and Adaptive Computer Technology (AAACT) team will highlight the accessibility features that are built into Android and iOS devices and that can be used on your Government of Canada-issued smartphone.

    The team will also give a brief overview of the types of adaptive technology in use on GC computers and how they assist in removing barriers to accessibility.

    Participants will learn about:

    • accessibility features on iOS and Android smartphones
    • accessibility features built into Windows 10 and Microsoft 365
    • other accessibility tools for Windows 10 that are available in the marketplace
    • non-IT solutions to removing accessibility barriers, such as furniture, lighting and so on

    The AAACT team will answer questions from participants and, upon request, is prepared to demonstrate how most of the adaptive technology works.

    If you have any questions, please contact the AAACT program.
